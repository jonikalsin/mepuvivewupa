Kenny Rogers Biography

Kenneth Donald Rogers was born in Houston, Texas, on August 21, 1938. His father, Edward Floyd Rogers, was a carpenter who died in 1975, and his mother, Lucille Rogers, was also a nurse assistant who died in 1991. Kenny Rogers grew up in his hometown and attended the Wharton Dual Language Academy.

Career

Kenny Rogers began his career with the Bobby Doyle band, with whom he performed in clubs before splitting up in 1965. After that, he collaborated with two musicians, Eddy Arnold and Mickey Gilley. During his early career, Kenny Rogers was a member of another band called “The New Christy Minstrels.”

After “The New Christy Minstrels” failed, Kenny and the other members formed “The First Edition,” which they later renamed “Kenny Rogers and The First Edition.” They had many successful albums together before breaking up in 1976.

In the same year, 1976, Kenny Rogers launched his solo career with the release of “Love Lifted Me,” which charted on top country music charts. His self-titled album’s single “Lucille” reached number one on several charts around the world, including in the United Kingdom, Canada, Yugoslavia, and the United States. Kenny Rogers’ sixth album, “The Gambler,” debuted at number one on the Billboard Top Country Albums chart in the United States and at number one on the Canadian RPM Country Albums chart.

Kenny Rogers Achievements and Awards

Kenny Rogers has received over 60 awards and nominations. He was nominated for 19 Grammy Awards and won three of them. Kenny Rogers has two Grammy Awards for Best Male Country Vocal Performance for “The Gambler” and “Lucille,” as well as one for Best Country Collaboration with Vocals for “Make No Mistake, She’s Mine.”

Kenny Rogers has won 13 American Music Awards, seven Academy of Country Music Awards, five Country Music Association Awards, three Music City News Country Awards, and two CMT Music Awards. He was recently inducted into the Texas Country Music Hall of Fame in 2017.
