Rev Run | Run-DMC

Rev Run founded Run-DMC in 1983, along with the group members Darryl Mcdaniels and Jason Mizell. Run-DMC is considered one of the most popular and influential acts in the history of hip-hop culture.

Run-DMC became the first hip-hop group to achieve a gold record in 1984. It was also followed by the platinum-certified record King of Rock.

The group’s big breakthrough came in the year 1985 when they did a rap version of Aerosmith’s smash hit ‘Walk This Way. The song was a super hit, and still today, it is considered to be one of the most well-known rap songs.

However, in 2002, a tragedy befell the group when Jam Master Jay was shot and killed in his studio in Queens, New York.

In 2009, Run-D.M.C was inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame and became the second rap group to earn the honor.

In 2004, Rolling Stone ranked Run-DMC at No. 48 in its list of the 100 Greatest Artists of All Time. Similarly, in 2007, they were named ‘The Greatest Hip Hop Group of All Time’ by MTV and ‘Greatest Hip Hop Artist of All Time’ by VH1.

Run-DMC received the Grammy Lifetime Achievement Award in 2016. They have sold over 230 million records worldwide.

Rev Run | Career

Joseph Simmons began his music career in 1978. Before being part of Run-D.M.C., he was the lead vocalist in the hip-hop group ‘The Force’.

Simmons used Rev Run as his stage name.

He featured in an English pop band’s song Single 4 Lovers in 2005 as Rev Run for the first time.

In the same year, 2005, he released his first solo album, Distortion. It became a massive hit.

Rev Run also appeared on the MTV series “Run’s House,” a reality show revolving around his everyday life with his family from 2005-2009.

Similarly, he has starred in ‘Rev Run’s Sunday Suppers’ on the Cooking Channel, and ‘Rev Runs Around The World’ on the Travel Channel.

Rev Run | Charity

As of December 2023, Rev Run’s net worth is a staggering $60 million, and he loves giving back to the community.

Rev Run is one of the founders of the ‘The Rush Philanthropic Arts Foundation’ along with Russell Simmons and Danny Simmons.

The Rush Philanthropic Arts Foundation was founded in 1995 with the goal to fill the gap between people of diverse color faced in both accessing the arts and exhibition opportunities.

It is designed to inspire students to provide positive alternatives to high-risk behaviors and support academic performance.

Rush Education programs serve directly over 3,000 students yearly.
