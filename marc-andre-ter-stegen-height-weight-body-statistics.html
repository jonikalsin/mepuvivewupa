    Marc-André Ter Stegen facts

  • Ter Stegen started playing football in the youth categories of Borussia Mönchengladbach.
  • In the 2010/2011 season with Mönchengladbach, Ter Stegen was the star of the reserve team. He was also included in the first-team squad in several games but eventually didn’t get a chance to play.
  • On January 6, 2014, he refused to renew his contract with Mönchengladbach as he was previously linked with Spanish club FC Barcelona.
  • On May 22, 2014, Ter Stegen signed a new five-year deal with FC Barcelona worth €12 million with a €80 million buyout clause.
  • He won the Best Save award for his save against Bayern Munich in the second game of the UEFA Champions League semifinals.
  • He won the UEFA European Under-17 Championship with Germany.
  • Ter Stegen was seen as Germany’s reserve goalkeeper at Euro 2016.
