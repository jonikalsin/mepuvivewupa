How long did Mike McCarthy Coach the Green Bay Packers?

 Posted on May 23, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  318 words  |  Martina Birk

Mike McCarthy spent 13 years as the head coach of the Green Bay Packers before being fired in December 2018. During his tenure, McCarthy led the Packers to the playoffs in nine out of the 13 seasons, including a victory in Super Bowl XLV in 2010. Here's a detailed breakdown of his coaching career with the Green Bay Packers:

Years as Head Coach

  • Mike McCarthy was the head coach of the Green Bay Packers for 13 seasons, from 2006 to 2018.
  • He was hired on January 2, 2006, replacing Mike Sherman, who left the Packers after a 4-12 season in 2005.

Achievements and Records

  • McCarthy's overall record with the Packers was 125-77-2, with a .618 winning percentage.
  • In the playoffs, his record was 10-8 with Green Bay, and his ten playoff wins are the most by any Green Bay head coach.

Yearly Performance

McCarthy's yearly record with the Packers was as follows:

  • 2006: 8-8
  • 2007: 13-3
  • 2008: 6-10
  • 2009: 11-5
  • 2010: 10-6
  • 2011: 15-1
  • 2012: 11-5
  • 2013: 8-7-1
  • 2014: 12-4
  • 2015: 10-6
  • 2016: 10-6
  • 2017: 7-9
  • 2018: 4-7-1 (fired after 13 games)

Transition at Quarterback

  • During his time with the Packers, McCarthy oversaw the transition at quarterback from Brett Favre to Aaron Rodgers, who became his quarterback in 2008 and won a Super Bowl.

Player Achievements

  • Several players excelled under his leadership, with awards given to Charles Woodson for Defensive Player of the Year in 2009, Aaron Rodgers winning MVP in 2011 and 2014, and Eddie Lacy winning Offensive Rookie of the Year in 2013.

Overall Impact

  • McCarthy's impact on the team was significant, as he led the Packers to multiple successful seasons and guided the team to the ultimate success by winning Super Bowl XLV in 2010.

Mike McCarthy's tenure as head coach of the Green Bay Packers spanned 13 seasons, during which he achieved considerable success and left a lasting impact on the team's history.
